Blood Flows

I intended to produce an image of blood flowing throughout ones face. It glows with life, pulsating with every moment as new thoughts are produced and emotions are expressed. The glass mannequin head serves as a perfect medium for the light to shine through, signifying the irresistible permeability of all human faces. The pulse of life can be seen through anyones face, and my photo demonstrates this profound concept to its core.

  • 55mm
  • ƒ/5.6

Shining Cold

I live for low-key, snow-based photography. The way the moonlight gently reflects off the snow as it glistens from an angle is to die for, and I feel that this shot serves as a baseline for such an effect. By contrasting the warm, man-made light with the natural, cold light of the moon, a strange dichotomy in the viewers mind is produced. The artifice becomes more appealing than the natural world itself.

  • 18mm
  • ƒ/7.1

Breath of Bucephalus

Ive always had an ethereal connection to the equine. Its my strange, neverending fascination. My house is littered with icons of horses and all that they entail, as my mother was an award-winning horseback rider and former horse owner years ago. This dark, brooding porcelain steeds face helps encapsulate my awe of the animal. Combined with the flares of bokeh light behind it, an image of incredible shock and mysticism is produced. These are majestic creatures, objects of both beauty and supreme utility. I seek to celebrate the horses ideal form, as its an ideal that all humans ought to internalize and actualize in every sense of the word.

  • 55mm
  • ƒ/5.6
  • 1/3
  • 400

Flight of the Obelisk

Aviation is a fundamental pillar of my life. My grandfather and father were both hobbyist pilots, and I have always sought to join their ranks of fast-flying aces. Shown here is the air traffic control tower at the Smithsonian Air and Space Museum Hangar in Dulles. Within its halls are the most potent machines of war, responsible for the destruction of countless lives and destabilization of nations throughout the world. Both Axis and Ally aircraft rest upon the displays, staring at each other as to say the war shall never be over. But outside of the gasoline-fueled trophy gallery is a beautiful, minimalist facade with this tower reigning above. The shades are simple, the lines are clearly defined, and the towers function is to know all who pass by. Perhaps we need more all-knowing towers than weapons of war, ones who survey the peaceful lands and take in the beauty of a world without conflict. Flight is incredibly dear to me. To see it wasted on war is truly an inner death.

  • 18mm
  • ƒ/11
  • 1/320
  • 200

Steam powered

The industrial revolution. The lower bound of the era of progress we call the civilized world. The Enlightenment-minded academics harken back to it with an overwhelming sense of nostalgia and gratitude, conveniently brushing past the incomprehensible suffering that resulted. Slums, uneducated children bound to coal mines, every form of poverty you can possibly imagine. This isnt just a rendering of a video game controller. This is a representation of everything that comes when mankind ignores his agrarian roots and attempts to become his own steam-powered god. Our modern representation of this transition is depicted in a sleek, glorious fashion as shown here. Its clean, free of all the human torment that the industrialized world granted us. Although we congratulate ourselves for harnessing more advanced forms of machinery, our power is still derived from steam. Steam is our ultimate controller, and we are wholly complicit.

  • Canon EOS Rebel XTi
  • 150mm
  • ƒ/8
  • 1/15
  • 100

Lucas Igel

From a sense made not of our atoms.

I was born in Minneapolis near the closing of the 20th century, a period riddled with the most horrific evils man could ever muster. But ever since I've been conscious, I have come to know a new era, one characterized by the endless promise of novelty and shiny electronic gadgets. This fast-paced, interconnected world has taken its toll on every one of its inhabitants, and my work seeks to set the record straight. I capture beauty on Earth in its purest form, straight from my camera's sensor to your eyes. My work screams of order, consistency, and riddance from the daily hustle of this late-stage capitalist oligarchy we call a nation.
