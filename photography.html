Blood Flows

I intended to produce an image of blood flowing throughout one’s face. It glows with life, pulsating with every moment as new thoughts are produced and emotions are expressed. The glass mannequin head serves as a perfect medium for the light to shine through, signifying the irresistible permeability of all human faces. The pulse of life can be seen through anyone’s face, and my photo demonstrates this profound concept to its core.

  • Canon EOS Rebel XTi
  • 55mm
  • ƒ/5.6
  • 1
  • 100

Shining Cold

I live for low-key, snow-based photography. The way the moonlight gently reflects off the snow as it glistens from an angle is to die for, and I feel that this shot serves as a baseline for such an effect. By contrasting the warm, man-made light with the natural, cold light of the moon, a strange dichotomy in the viewer’s mind is produced. The artifice becomes more appealing than the natural world itself.

  • Canon EOS Rebel XTi
  • 18mm
  • ƒ/7.1
  • 1/6
  • 200

Breath of Bucephalus

I’ve always had an ethereal connection to the equine. It’s my strange, neverending fascination. My house is littered with icons of horses and all that they entail, as my mother was an award-winning horseback rider and former horse owner years ago. This dark, brooding porcelain steed’s face helps encapsulate my awe of the animal. Combined with the flares of bokeh light behind it, an image of incredible shock and mysticism is produced. These are majestic creatures, objects of both beauty and supreme utility. I seek to celebrate the horse’s ideal form, as it’s an ideal that all humans ought to internalize and actualize in every sense of the word.

  • Canon EOS Rebel XTi
  • 55mm
  • ƒ/5.6
  • 1/3
  • 400
